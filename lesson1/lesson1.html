Glossary Terms

Botanist

A scientist who studies plants.

Diagnose

To identify what is wrong with a living thing by carefully examining it and looking for clues about what physical condition may be missing or out of balance.

Function

The job or role that a part of a plant performs to help it survive.

Germinate

When a seed begins to sprout and grow into a new plant after absorbing water and being exposed to the right temperature and conditions.

Investigate

To carefully examine and gather information about something in order to find an answer or understand it better.

Nutrients

Substances found in soil and water that plants absorb through their roots to grow and stay healthy.

Observe / Observation

Information gathered by using your senses—sight, hearing, touch, smell, or taste—to carefully notice and describe something in the world around you.

Structure

A physical part of a plant, such as the roots, stem, leaves, flower, or fruit, that has a specific job.

How can we turn our wonderings into testable questions?

What is a testable question?

A testable question can be answered by changing one thing and measuring the effect it has on something else. It explores relationships between two different things.

Testable questions

  • Can be answered by doing an experiment.
  • Explore the relationship between two things.
  • One thing is changed and its effect on another thing is measured.
  • Different scientists should reach a similar answer when conducting the same fair test.

Untestable questions

  • May be answered using personal opinions, values or beliefs.
  • Do not investigate the effect of changing one thing.
  • Cannot be answered using measurable evidence from an experiment.
  • Different people may provide different answers.

Look at the following examples from our See, Think, Wonder exercise.

Testable question

Does changing the angle of the ramp affect how far the car travels?

✓
Is something being changed?

Yes. The angle of the ramp is changed.

✓
Can the effect be measured?

Yes. Measure the distance the car travels.

Untestable question

Does changing the angle of the ramp affect how much fun the car has?

✓
Is something being changed?

Yes. The angle of the ramp is changed.

×
Can the effect be measured?

No. Fun is based on personal opinion.

Testable question

What happens to the distance a car travels if we change the size of the car?

✓
Is something being changed?

Yes. The size of the car is changed.

✓
Can the effect be measured?

Yes. Measure the distance the car travels.

Untestable question

How far does the red car travel?

×
Is something being changed?

No. Nothing is changed.

✓
Can the effect be measured?

Yes. The distance the car travels can be measured.

How do we write testable questions?

A testable question describes what is being changed and what is being measured. Testable questions can begin with words such as What, How, Does and If.

Question structures

What will changing do to ?
How does changing affect ?
Does changing affect ?
If we change , how does it affect ?
