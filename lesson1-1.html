Lesson 1: Structure & Function of a Plant

Glossary Terms

Botanist

A scientist who studies plants.

Diagnose

To identify what is wrong with a living thing by carefully examining it and looking for clues about what physical condition may be missing or out of balance.

Function

The job or role that a part of a plant performs to help it survive.

Germinate

When a seed begins to sprout and grow into a new plant after absorbing water and being exposed to the right temperature and conditions.

Investigate

To carefully examine and gather information about something in order to find an answer or understand it better.

Nutrients

Substances found in soil and water that plants absorb through their roots to grow and stay healthy.

Observe / Observation

Information gathered by using your senses—sight, hearing, touch, smell, or taste—to carefully notice and describe something in the world around you.

Structure

A physical part of a plant, such as the roots, stem, leaves, flower, or fruit, that has a specific job.

Lesson 1

What do plants need to survive?

Plants are living organisms found growing all around the world. Animals rely on plants for food, nutrients and the oxygen they produce.

Your mission

Become a botanist

In this lesson, you will become a plant detective. You will investigate why plants become unhealthy and work out how to make them healthy again.

Learning objective

Identify the physical conditions that affect a plant’s ability to grow and survive.

Success criteria

  1. I can identify the physical conditions that a plant needs to grow and survive.
  2. I can compare healthy and unhealthy plants to identify which physical condition to change to make it healthy again.

Think like a scientist

See, Think, Wonder

Botanists observe how plants grow and behave. They use a thinking routine called See, Think, Wonder to make scientific observations.

        Why do plants need the right physical conditions to survive?

        Some of the physical conditions that plants need to grow include sunlight, soil, water, carbon dioxide, oxygen, nutrients, and temperature. Use the diagram and interactive video to investigate why they need these factors. You will also explore the function of different parts of the plant.

        Can you solve the mystery of the unhealthy plants?

        Botanists, farmers, nursery workers and gardeners often need to be plant detectives to solve the mystery of unhealthy plants. They think about the physical conditions that plants need to be healthy and how too much or too little of each factor can cause a plant to become unhealthy. Use your detective skills to make observations and come up with an idea for what each plant might need to become healthy again. Test your idea and see if you can solve the mystery for each unhealthy plant.
