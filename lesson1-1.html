Vocabulary List

Stem

The stem holds the plant upright and carries water and nutrients to different parts of the plant.

Bud

A bud is a small growth on a plant that can develop into a leaf, flower, or new shoot.

Roots

Roots hold the plant in the soil and absorb water and nutrients from the ground.

Leaf

A leaf captures sunlight and helps the plant make food.

Flower

A flower is the part of some plants that helps the plant make seeds.

Seed

A seed can grow into a new plant when it has the right conditions.

Lesson 1

What do plants need to survive?

Plants are living organisms found growing all around the world. Animals rely on plants for food, nutrients and the oxygen they produce.

Your mission

Become a botanist

In this lesson, you will become a plant detective. You will investigate why plants become sick and work out how to make them healthy again.

Learning objective

Identify the physical conditions that affect a plant’s ability to grow and survive.

Success criteria

  1. I can identify the physical conditions that a plant needs to grow and survive.
  2. I can compare healthy and unhealthy plants to identify which physical condition needs to change to treat the unhealthy plant.

Think like a scientist

See, Think, Wonder

Botanists observe how plants grow and behave. They use a thinking routine called See, Think, Wonder to make scientific observations.
